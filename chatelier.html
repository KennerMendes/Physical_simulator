Le Chatelier's Principle

This principle claims that if you disturb a chemical equilibrium, that reaction will be accelerated towards relieving the stressor. The simulation below represents the following chemical equilibrium:
A + B ⇌ C + D
Where green particles are A substance molecules, yellow particles are B, red particles are C and blue particles are D. When A and B collide with enough velocity (in this simplified simulation) they become C and D and vice versa. Realize at initial situation you, at most of the time, see 3 particles of each substance. Click over the simulation to disturb the equilibrium adding more A particles and increasing its concentration.

When equilibrium is disturbed, it advances towards reducing A concentration. As a result, direct reaction will be accelerated, decreasing A and B concentrations and increasing C and D concentrations. Realize when the new equilibrium is reached, at the most of time, you see 1 yellow particle (B substance) indicating the described phenomenom happened.
