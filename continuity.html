Continuity Equation

Continuity equation only says when you strangle some flow, fluid velocity will increase after the tightness (to keep the flow constant).
The simulation below is a simplified representation of this phenomenon. We will use a particle system rather than fluid itself, the particle's color refers to its horizontal velocity (flow direction): green for very slow, red for very fast and yellow for moderate. To tight the flow, put mouse over simulation and press mouse button; to free the flow, only release the button.

In the normal flow (no tightness) particles are predominantly yellow. When you strangle this flow, a lot of particles before tightness become green and after tightness, some particles become red, indicating avarage flow velocity after tightness is higher then before.
Thinking about space, this phenomenon makes sense too. Before tightness there is space enough to a lot of particles pass throug at the same time, after tightness there is space for only one, so it is pretty much reasonable to admit particles before tightness will flow slower then after.
However, realize that the pressure (in our model it is represented by particles collisions) is higher before tightness than after (for more clarifications search for Bernoulli's equation). Realize too, our simulation is just a simplification with solid particles, not a real fluid, what means we don't heave constant "pressure", hence we will not have constant flow velocity, because of this you see some green particles after tightness too.
